Use these as adaptable patterns, not fixed templates. Replace bracketed items with the user's specific content.

## Establishing Territory

- [Topic] plays a central role in [field/application].
- Recent work has increasingly focused on [topic/problem].
- [Phenomenon] is important because it affects [outcome/process].
- In [field], [method/material/system] is widely used to [purpose].

## Narrowing Focus

- A key challenge in [area] is [specific problem].
- For [specific system/population/context], [issue] remains difficult to resolve.
- This is particularly important when [condition/context].
- Among these factors, [specific factor] is critical because [reason].

## Summarizing Prior Work

- Previous studies have shown that [finding].
- Several approaches have been proposed to [aim].
- [Author/group] demonstrated that [specific result].
- Existing work has mainly examined [scope].

## Identifying A Gap Or Need

- However, less is known about [specific unknown].
- The extent to which [relationship] remains unclear.
- Few studies have examined [topic] under [condition].
- Existing methods do not fully address [limitation].
- It remains challenging to [task] because [reason].

## Stating Aim Or Contribution

- This study aims to [objective].
- Here, we investigate [question] using [method/data].
- We develop/evaluate/compare [object] to [purpose].
- The main contribution of this work is [contribution].
- By [approach], this study provides [type of evidence/insight].

## Describing Methods

- [Procedure] was performed to [purpose].
- [Material/data] was selected because [rationale].
- To minimize [bias/error], we [action].
- [Variable] was measured using [instrument/method].
- [Analysis] was conducted to determine whether [question].

## Reporting Results

- [Outcome] increased/decreased from [value] to [value].
- Compared with [baseline/group], [condition] showed [difference].
- The results indicate/show/suggest that [finding].
- No significant difference was observed between [groups].
- A similar pattern was observed for [secondary measure].
- In contrast, [exception/different pattern] was found in [condition].

## Interpreting Results

- These findings suggest that [interpretation].
- One possible explanation is that [mechanism].
- This pattern may reflect [process], rather than [alternative].
- Together, these results support [claim].
- The finding is consistent with [prior work], which reported [comparison].

## Calibrating Claim Strength

Stronger evidence:

- These results demonstrate that [claim].
- The data show that [claim].

Moderate evidence:

- These results indicate that [claim].
- The findings suggest that [claim].

Preliminary or indirect evidence:

- These results may indicate that [claim].
- This pattern is consistent with [possible interpretation].
- Further work is needed to determine whether [claim].

Avoid making causal claims unless the design supports causality.

## Limitations

- This study has several limitations.
- First, [limitation] may affect [interpretation/generalizability].
- Because [constraint], the results should be interpreted with caution.
- Future studies should examine [next step] in [context].

## Consistency And Clarity

Prefer stable terminology. If the paper introduces "framework", avoid switching to "methodology", "approach", and "model" unless those terms mean different things.

Use sentence openings to guide the reader:

- To test this hypothesis, ...
- To evaluate the robustness of this result, ...
- Consistent with this interpretation, ...
- In contrast to [previous finding], ...
- Taken together, ...
